Samburu

Welcome To Samburu

Samburu National Reserve, situated in northern Kenya, is a hidden gem renowned for its rugged landscapes and unique wildlife. This semi-arid wilderness is home to species rarely found elsewhere, such as the reticulated giraffe, Grevy's zebra, and the Somali ostrich. Visitors to Samburu can embark on thrilling game drives through the reserve's diverse terrain, which includes acacia forests, riverine woodlands, and open plains. The Ewaso Ng'iro River, a lifeline for the region, attracts wildlife like elephants, crocodiles, and hippos, offering exceptional opportunities for wildlife viewing. Beyond its natural wonders, Samburu is also rich in cultural heritage, with the Samburu people inhabiting the area for centuries. Travelers can immerse themselves in their traditions, learning about their nomadic lifestyle, colorful attire, and unique customs through cultural visits and interactions.
